Main Screen

When you choose the game mode "Survivor", the image below is the screen that will appear. Your character will be on the far right, closest to you.

In the top left, there should be an icon with a human silouette on it. Click on it, and it will take you to your character selection.

Character Selection

This screen lists all options for the characters that the game provides. Some of the characters are free, while others require in-game and real-world currency to purchase. When you want to choose a character, click on their icon, and a green check mark will appear on their icon in the character list. NOTE: You CAN own multiple characters. Once you have a character to Prestige level 1, you can share their perks with other characters you own. I will touch on this more when I talk about the character Perks . Your prestige level is the number at the bottom left of Aestri's icon in the character selection menu. Prestiging will be discussed when I talk about the Blood Web .

Customizations

The Customizatons tag allows you to edit how your character looks. You can choose a whole outfit, or can customize the pieces of the outfit. You can also choose charms, which are optional.

When choosing your character's outfit, keep in mind you want to blend into your environment. You will be hiding from a killer, trying to repair generators. It will be hard to hide if you have a brightly-colored outfit (I have personal experience with this). While designing a character's Outfit to be exactly what you want sounds fun, it is not recommended, especially for players that aren't experts at the game (I am not an expert, either).

Blood Web

The blood web is a way for a user to buy perks, offerings, items, and item add-ons for their character. As you advance through the blood web, The Entity will start to take over and prevent you from purchasing some of the items. The Entity is there to make you think strategically about what items, perks, or offerings you want that are available in the blood web. Think carefully!
You can also buy different levels (rarity) of items, perks, and offerings.

  • Pink: Ultra Rare
  • Purple: Very Rare
  • Blue: Rare
  • Green: Uncommon
  • Brown: Common

This is what the blood web will look like when you open it (after opening it for the first time).

There are different blood webs for each character, so items/perks/offerings ("items") bought for one character via the blood web is not shared between all other owned characters. You will have to purchase those items in the blood web for your other characters if you want them to have the same items. By playing the game, you will earn Blood Points, which is the currency used for your Blood Web. To purchase an item, simply click on the item(s) you want. Before purchasing an item(s), you can hover over it, and a small pop-up will tell you information about the item you are hovering over. In the bottom right corner of the pop-up, the required blood points to spend will be shown to purchase the item. One more thing I'd like to touch on is Prestiging. Prestiging allows you to share perks of a character with other characters you own (which those characters will have to purchase in their own blood web). To prestige, you must make it through 50 levels of the blood web. You can advance levels by purchasing (most to all) items in the blood web level. Once you prestige, your prestige level will appear within a circle at the top left of your character menu (see the previous image). It will also be shown at the bottom left of your character icon when you go to your character selection menu.
